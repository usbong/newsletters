>　塾に通わず米ハーバード大へ　廣津留すみれさんが明かす勉強法

Ms. Sumire Hirotsuru shares her studying technique to get into Harvard University without going to a review center

> 聞き手 編集委員・宮坂麻子

Reporter/Editor: MIYASAKI, Asako

> 2025年12月16日 16時00分

2025-12-16T16:00

## --

>　受験生のみなさんは、受験勉強も佳境の時期だと思います。苦しい時はぜひ、入学後のキラキラした学生生活ややりたいことに挑戦している自分を想像してください。

I believe that now is the crucial time to be studying for the entrance exams. During times of hardship, please imagine yourselves living the glamorous student life that you'll be enjoying upon entering university and taking on the challenges that you'd want to face.

>　2歳でバイオリン、5歳でピアノを始めました。「歯磨き、ご飯、バイオリン」というくらい、弾くのは当たり前で、一生続けるものと自然に思っていました。大分から福岡や東京の先生にも習いに行っていました。

I began the violin when I was two years old, and the piano, when I was five. "Brushing my teeth, eating food, playing the violin." The last one was so natural to me that I thought that I'll be continuing on with it my whole life. For the most part, I went to learn from teachers in Fukuoka and Tokyo. 

>　行き当たりばったり、いつも目の前のことを楽しむ方なので、音大進学を勧められることはありましたが、高校に入っても音楽の道へ進むとか東大をめざすとか考えなかったですね。塾に通ったこともないから、偏差値には無縁でしたし。

As a person who simply goes with the flow and just enjoys what is in front of me, I was advised to go to music school for further studies. However, I didn't really think about whether to go to music school or aim for the University of Tokyo when I entered High School. I had never gone to a review center, and this wasn't because I'm deviant.

>　母も楽観的で、何を相談しても「OK!　いいんじゃない」と。「そんなのあたり前田のクラッカー!」とギャグで返され、進路をとやかく言うこともなかった。

My mother, also being optimistic, encouraged me with, "OK! Isn't that great?" whenever I talked to her about these things. She'd crack up a joke like "That's obvious as rice crackers," without telling me which path to take.

## --

> 「10分間で50単語」の猛勉強

"50 words in 10 minutes"-type of diligence

## --

>　「この大学へ行きたい」と思ったのは、県立大分上野丘高校2年の時。思い切って応募した海外オーディションで勝ち取った全米ツアーの後、せっかくだからと大学のキャンパスも巡りました。その時、ハーバード大学でいろんな活動をする学生たちを知り、「ここなら音楽も学業も両立できるかも」と思ったんです。

I was in second year at the Oita Ueno Public High School when I thought that "I wanted to go to this university." After a nationwide U.S. tour that I won by taking the leap and auditioning overseas, I went around and visited the university campus thinking that I might as well. I was already there. At the time, I met students who were involved in various activities at Harvard University. I thought that "I could do both academics and music together if I studied here."

>　高校の先生も友達も海外大進学の知識はないし、ネットで調べて、そこから記憶にないほど猛勉強しました。英語は、物心ついた時からクイズ感覚で楽しんで、勉強という感覚はあまりありませんでしたが、受験では苦労しました。

My high school teachers and friends didn't know much about pursuing higher education abroad, so I searched online, and studied harder than I ever had from that point onward. Since as far back as I could remember, I enjoyed studying English for the quiz-like feel to it, which felt like I was hardly studying at all. However, I struggled with this subject when I took Harvard's entrance exam.

>　米国のSAT（大学進学適性試験）で入学できる高得点を取るには、2万語の英単語を覚えなきゃならない。「10分間で50語覚える」とタスクを決めて、必死で覚えました。タイマーをかけて、時間で区切らないと、いつまでもかかっちゃって勉強の精度も上がらないので、時間を区切ることは心がけていましたね。

To be able to get outstanding SAT marks that would be high enough to let a student enter university, he must remember 20,000 English vocabulary words. I did this task diligently once I decided to take on the challenge of "memorizing 50 words in 10 minutes." Not marking my time with a timer wouldn't increase my academic performance, because I'd end up taking too much time, so I made sure that I took note of my time well.

>　あと、出題も英語なので、数学の「階乗」などの用語や世界史の「チンギス・ハーン」などの人名の表記を英語で覚えるのも苦労しました。

Next, because the questions were also in English, I struggled with memorizing special math vocabulary with words like "factorial" as well as the list of names, which noted famous people like "Genghis Khan" and others.

>　米国の大学入試は日本と大きく異なり、受験で重視されるポイント、各科目での文化の違いなど、目からうろこでした。

The university entrance exams in the U.S. are very different from those in Japan, especially in terms of the areas that the exams focus on, and it was an eye-opener for me to learn about the cultural differences present in the subjects.

## --

> 国内難関大も受験

Taking the entrance exams at the local universities too

## --

>　高校の東大クラスの文系の在籍だったので、2次試験の対策問題の添削も毎日受けなければならず、朝は苦手でギリギリに起きて登校、放課後は教室で友達と教えあって。宿題は学校で終わらせました。家に帰ったらバイオリンを少し弾いて、深夜まで勉強。半年ぐらいバイオリンを弾く時間もなかった。

Since I was enrolled in the University of Tokyo's literature class during high school, I had to take reviews for the university's second exam. While I'm not good at waking up early, I still managed to go to class. After which, my friend and I would remain inside the classroom and teach each other the subject matter. We'd finish the homeworks in school. When I returned home, I'd play a little bit of violin, and would study until late in the evening. For about half a year, I didn't have any more time to play the violin.

>　ハーバードでやりたいことがたくさんあって、そのことを考えていましたが、受かるとは思っていなかったので、AO入試で慶応大学総合政策学部を受けました。ハーバード大は、早期出願で12月には合格が出ましたが、進学校の高校から全員共通テストも受けるよう言われていたので受けて、渡米する秋まで慶応に通っていました。もし、ハーバードに不合格だったら、年明けの出願でほかのアメリカの大学も出願していたかな。

There are so many things that I'd want to do at Harvard, and while I often mused about those things, I didn't really think that I'd pass the entrance exam, so I took the entrance exam of Keio University's Department of Policy Studies. By December, Harvard University sent out the list of names of the passers who submitted their applications early. However, I still took the entrance exams at Keio University, because we were told that everyone at our high school had to take the common entrance exam. This is why I went to Keio University until I moved to the United States in the fall. If I didn't pass Harvard University, I'd probably submit an application at another university in the U.S. when the new year began.

>　ハーバードでは、友達に誘われて2年生で応用数学を専攻しました。数列と音楽ってつながるから何か融合した学びができるかもしれないな、と。でもちょっと違うなと感じて、3年生で社会学へ。音楽を主専攻、グローバルヘルスを副専攻にしたのは、4年生からです。人の可能性って200%にも広げられるから、興味のあることはどんどん挑戦すればいいと思います。

At Harvard, I majored in Applied Mathematics during my sophomore year after a friend invited me. I thought that I could study something that combined together sequences of numbers and music. But I felt that it was a little different from what I imagined, so I shifted to Sociology during my junior year. It was in senior year when I majored in Music and made my secondary major Global Health. I believe that people could grow their potential up to 200%, so we should all take on more and more challenges that interest us.

>　ハーバードは、挙手してから意見を考える人もいるくらいみんなが活発に発言して議論します。食堂でもそれぞれの専門の話をして、「え、おもしろそう」と。

At Harvard, there are actually people who raise their hands and are very opinionated. They actively speak out and discuss these things. At the dining hall, people talk about various issues using technical vocabulary, and I thought that "they sounded interesting."

## --

> 「自分で限界を作らないで」

"Don't create limitations for yourself."

## --

>　学業以外も盛んで、私も、学生オペラ、弦楽アンサンブル、オーケストラ、ジャパンソサエティーの4団体で活動しました。オーケストラでは、ヨルダンやイスラエルにも演奏に出かけて、刺激を受けました。

Other than academics, there are plenty of things to do, and I myself was active in four organizations, namely Student Opera, Percussion Ensemble, Orchestra and Japan Society. In the Orchestra, I went out to perform in Jordan and Israel. I was thrilled.

>　一度、きちんと音楽教育を受けたくて大学院はニューヨークのジュリアード音楽院へ。朝からずっと演奏できる環境はそれはそれで幸せでした。街もボストンとはまた違う刺激をくれました。

Then, I wanted to take a proper music education so much that I went to the New York Juilliard School of Music for Graduate School. I was happy to be able to study in an environment where I could perform continuously from the morning onward. The streets were also different from Boston's so I was again thrilled to be there.

>　受験を目の前にしている人にも、これから受験生になる人にも、自分で限界を作らないで、と伝えたいです。何でも不可能なことはない。

I'd like to tell people who are considering taking the entrance exam, or those who'll be taking it soon that they shouldn't create limitations for themselves. Anything is possible.

>　結果的に、第1志望に進めなかったとしても、自分で与えられた環境を生かす方法って、絶対あると思う。大学のレベルや所属がすべてじゃないし、めざすべきは世界だから。ポケモンカードの国際大会で入賞した学生がいますが、学業以外に世界に通じるものを持ったっていい。

As a result, even if you don't get into your first choice, I think that there's definitely a way to improve your present condition. It's not all about the university's ranking or its affiliations after all. In other words, your aim should be global. While one of you may have won an award at an International Pokémon Trading Card Tournament, the point is that you should have something that can communicate with the world other than academics.

>　音大進学を反対されて、断念した人もいるかもしれません。でも、音楽は一生つきあうもので他の学部だからできなくなるわけじゃない。国際教養大学では「マクロ音楽学」という名のもと、「音楽×脳」「音楽×教育」など、「音楽×○○」の授業を教えています。音楽を究めることも素晴らしいけれど、かけ算で融合して音楽とかかわる道もあります。

There might be people who've given up after getting no encouragement to go to music school. But music is something that you pursue your whole life; it isn't something that you can't do alongside another academic field. In Akita International University, there are classes that teach "Music x Brain," "Music x Education," "Music x something" that are based on "Macro Music."  While specializing in music is wonderful, there's also a way to combine multiplication with music.

>　受験勉強がいまの私に影響していることは特にないですが、大学での学びや友達につながったことは大きかったです。

Even if studying for the entrance exams has no particular impact on me right now, studying at the university and the friends whom I met there have.

>　これから受験生になるみなさんも、Anything is possible!!

To all who'll be taking the entrance exams, "Anything is possible!!"

> 略歴

Brief Biography

## --

>　ひろつる・すみれ　1993年大分県生まれ。米ハーバード大卒、米ジュリアード音楽院修士課程修了。バイオリニストとして年60公演以上を行う傍ら国際教養大特任准教授、成蹊大客員准教授、文部科学省中央教育審議会委員なども務める。9月にソロアルバム「11　Stories」を発売。来年1月に広島、宮崎、北海道、2月に島根、佐賀、3月に東京、奈良などで演奏会予定。

Sumire Hirotsuru was born in Oita Prefecture in 1993. She graduated from Harvard University and completed her master's degree in the Juilliard Music School. As a violinist, she has performed in front of a public audience over 60 times, and is currently a Special Appointed Associate Professor at the Akita International University, a Guest Associate Professor at the Seikei University, as well as a Member of the Central Education Council, Ministry of Education, Culture, Sports, Science and Technology. In September, she sold her solo album, "11 Stories." Next year in January, she plans to perform in Hiroshima, Miyazaki, Hokkaido, in February, Shimane, Saga, and in March, Tokyo and Nara, among many others.
