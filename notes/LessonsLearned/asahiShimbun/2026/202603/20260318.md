> 魚は健康にいい、ただ選び方も大切　血中の水銀濃度が糖尿病に関係か

Diabetes connected to mercury concentration in the blood: "Fish is good for a person's health, but choosing which fish is also important"

> 2026年3月16日 16時00分

2026-03-16T16:00

> 田村建二

TAMURA, Kenji

## --

>　魚を食べることは、何かと健康にいいと考えられている。ただ、どんな魚をどれだけ口にするか、少しだけ気にしたほうがいいのかも知れない。

It's believed that eating fish has some kind of benefit toward a person's health. But it might also be good to be a little bit particular about which type of fish and how much of it should be consumed.

>　食べものの中でも魚に比較的多く含まれる重金属の水銀が、血液中に多めの人は、2型糖尿病を発症しやすいという研究結果が論文報告（https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clnu.2025.106563別ウインドウで開きます）された。

Among food types, fish relatively have plenty of the metal, mercury. Results from research ([https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clnu.2025.106563](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.clnu.2025.106563)) show that it's easy for people who have plenty of this mercury in their blood to have an onset of Type 2 Diabetes.

## --

> 人間ドック受診者を5年追跡

Tracking people for five years after undergoing a medical check-up

## --

>　国立健康危機管理研究機構（JIHS）や国立環境研究所などのチームが研究に取り組んだ。

The team from the Japan Institute for Health Security (JIHS), the National Institute for Environmental Studies and others worked on this research study.  

>　2008年度に人間ドックを受け、その時点で糖尿病でなかった勤労者約4800人（男性が約9割）に血液を提供してもらい、その後を5年間追跡。2型糖尿病になった325人と、ならなかった人のうち年齢や性別などをあわせた611人について、2008年度時点での血中の水銀濃度などとの関連を分析した。

In 2008, about 4,800 workers (about 90% male), who at the time didn't have diabetes, underwent a complete medical check-up, had their blood taken, and were tracked for five years after the examination. The 325 people who got Type 2 Diabetes and those who didn't totaled 611 people when grouped based on age, sex, and other factors, and were part of the research study conducted in 2008 to analyze the link between diabetes and mercury concentration in the blood.

>　分析にあたっては、水銀の血中濃度の程度に応じて四つのグループに分け、喫煙や肥満度、身体活動量や糖尿病の家族歴などが影響しないよう統計的に調整した。

With respect to the analysis portion of the study, the research team grouped the respondents into four based on the level of mercury concentration in their blood, and studied the numbers statistically without being influenced by factors like smoking, obesity, amount of physical activity and family history of diabetes.

>　すると、血中濃度が最も高いグループは最も低いグループに比べ、2型糖尿病を発症した人の割合が1.98倍高かったという。

When the group with the highest mercury concentration in their blood was compared to the group with the lowest, it was found that they got Type 2 Diabetes 1.98 times more.

## --

> 水銀が少なめの魚は?

Fish with low mercury?

## --

>　水銀の影響で、血液中の糖を処理するインスリンというホルモンの分泌機能が低下することで、糖尿病になりやすくなる可能性が推測されている。日本人では、食事由来の水銀のうち、約90%を魚介類からとっているという。

Because of mercury, secretion of "insulin," the hormone which processes sugar in the blood, goes down, and the possibility of getting diabetes is foreseen to be much higher. Among Japanese people, mercury from food is reported to come from about 90% of seafood.

>　同じような関連は、グリーンランドのイヌイットの人たちを対象とした研究でも報告されている。魚や海産哺乳類をよく食べ、水銀の血中濃度が高めとされる人たちだ。

In a similar and related research study, the respondents studied were the Inuit of Greenland, a people who eat plenty of fish and marine mammals, and are known to have high levels of mercury concentration in the blood. 

>　ただ魚は、たんぱく質はもちろん、血管や脳の健康維持に大切な脂肪酸や、骨の健康に必要なビタミンDなどを豊富に含む。このため研究チームの伊東葵・JIHS上級研究員は「魚を食べる習慣を保ちつつ、水銀の摂取量をなるべく少なくする工夫が大切です」と話す。

However, fish is abundant with, of course, protein, important fatty acids to maintain the health of blood vessels and the brain, as well as Vitamin D, which is necessary for bone health. For this reason, the research team's top brass from JIHS, Aoi Ito, says, "In maintaining the habit of eating fish, it's important to come up with a way to lessen, as much as possible, the amount of mercury taken by an individual from this food."

>　たとえば、メカジキやキンメダイ、クロマグロ、マカジキなどは水銀が比較的多く含まれる一方、キハダマグロやビンナガマグロ、カツオ、サケ、アジ、サバ、イワシ、サンマなどは含有量が少ないとされる。

For example, swordfish, splendid alfonsino, bluefin tuna, marlin and others have relatively plenty of mercury, while fish such as yellowfin tuna, albacore tuna, skipjack tuna, salmon, horse mackerel, mackerel, sardines and saury have low quantities of mercury.

## More

Since mercury is accumulated through the food chain starting from the plankton, then the small fish and finally to the big fish, generally speaking, the larger the fish, the easier for it to have plenty of mercury.

As to how much mercury is present in which type of fish, the Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare of Japan has information ([https://www.mhlw.go.jp/topics/bukyoku/iyaku/syoku-anzen/suigin/dl/050812-1-05.pdf](https://www.mhlw.go.jp/topics/bukyoku/iyaku/syoku-anzen/suigin/dl/050812-1-05.pdf)) on its website.

Furthermore, during the Food and Agriculture Organization (FAO) and the World Health Organization (WHO)'s experts panel meeting in 2003, the upper limit for mercury (methyl mercury) believed to have no effect in the health of a fetus and others among the ages of women capable of getting pregnant was presented to be "1.6 mg per week for every 1 kg of body weight." Therefore, a person with 60 kg of body weight could consume 96 mg per week.

In other words, when the amount of mercury contained in each fish, as stipulated on the Ministry's website, is computed, in the case of the swordfish, which has relatively plenty of mercury, a person with 60 kg of body weight could eat up to about 140 g per week without worry. Thus, a portion of 80 g could be eaten 1.5 times per week.

The same with the splendid alfonsino, it's about 180 g per week. Thus, up to 18 pieces of 10 g of sashimi could be consumed.

For an adult, in general, the amount that won't adversely impact his health is foreseen to go much higher than this. However, right now, there are still not a lot of research studies on this, so no specific figures are yet available for the general adult.
