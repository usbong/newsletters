> 進学費用の負担が重い…　低所得世帯、4割が借金で準備　民間調査

The heavy burden of financing continuing education, 40% of low-income households have prepared by taking on debt according to social survey

> 2026年3月4日 6時00分

2026-03-04T06:00

> 植松佳香

Yoshika Uematsu

## --

> 子どもの進学費用は低所得世帯でより負担が重いという調査結果を、子どもの貧困対策に取り組む公益財団法人あすのば（東京都港区）が公表した。高校無償化の拡大など教育費への公的支援が広がっているが、より手厚い取り組みを訴える。

Public Fund Group Asunoba (in Minato District, Tokyo City), which tackles poverty alleviation among children, has presented the results of a survey it conducted on the heavy burden of financing continuing education of children from low-income households. While public aid to finance children's education, which includes the proliferation of free high schools, has been increasing, more generous funding is still being sought.

>　調査は昨年10月に行った。対象は、子どもの入学・新生活を応援するあすのばの給付金を受給している住民税非課税世帯と生活保護世帯。オンラインで行い、813人の有効回答を得た。

The survey was conducted in October last year. The respondents were households exempt from resident tax and those under welfare receiving donations from Asunoba in order to help their children enter school or transition into a new life. Done online, the survey obtained valid responses from 813 people.  

>　結果によると、小中学校への入学や高校・大学への進学などで必要な費用を、親族や銀行などから借金をして用意した人が40.2%にのぼった。

According to the results of the survey, the number of people who've borrowed money from relatives, the bank and others in order to pay for the required fees to enter primary school or proceed to high school or college has risen to 40.2%. 

>　費用負担が大きいと感じるものは、制服・標準服61.6%、体操服47.4%、教科書や参考書39.6%、入学金39.2%、パソコン・タブレット端末33.1%の順で多かった（複数回答可）。

The things that were felt to be big financial burdens were, in descending order, school uniforms at 61.6%, P.E. attire at 47.4%, textbooks and reference materials at 39.6%, entrance exam fees at 39.2%, and PC/Tablet devices at 33.1%.

## More

Even at the free high schools, 50% said that they "first shouldered the payments" 

## --

Further, respondents who have children in high school were asked about their school fees. The country gave everyone (at most) 118,800 yen per year for school fees at public schools in 2025. Households with less than 5.9 million yen in annual income are given 396,000 yen for those with children at private schools.

However, 52.1% of the respondents said that they "first shouldered the payments" for school fees and others. A representative from Asunoba thinks, "This is due to variations in the schedule to seek for financial aid in order to pay for the school fees."

To reduce the financial burden of paying for the fees besides the school fees, Japan has been providing scholarships to low-income households. However, 10.2% of the respondents said that they don't know how to avail of it. 

Over 70% of the respondents said that the period when many scholarships are given, which is around December, is "late." Half of the respondents sought financial aid before entering school in January up to March. 

Even with financial aid, they have to temporarily shoulder the payments themselves to continue with their studies.

Asunoba's Director, Mr. Mitsuharu Ogawa, says, "Even with free education, those at the upper stratum in income receive the best benefits. Thus, it's important to give more generous aid to the stratum who are more in-need."
