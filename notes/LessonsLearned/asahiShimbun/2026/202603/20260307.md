> なぜ塩で食品が長持ちするの？　脱水と防腐ふたつの作用、先人の知恵

Why does salt help keep food last longer? Dehydration and food spoilage prevention are two of its uses according to the wisdom of the elders

## --

> 長崎県・藤田学さん（52）からの質問

Question from Manabu Fujita (52) of Nagasaki Prefecture

>　ののちゃん　ご近所さんがくれた塩サケを焼いたら、おばあちゃんが「昔の味だなぁ」って。

Nono-chan: After cooking the salted salmon I got from our neighbor, Grandma said, "It tastes like food from the past."

>　藤原先生　しょっぱかったんじゃない？

Teacher Fujiwara: Was it too salty?

>　のの　うん、ご飯をいっぱい食べちゃった。冷蔵庫がない時代は塩をたくさん使ったと言ってた。

Nono: Yes, she ate a lot of rice. She said that they had used plenty of salt during the time when there were still no refrigerators around.

>　先生　塩と保存の話だね。そもそも食べものが腐るというのは、もともと含んでいたり、外から付いたりした微生物が増えて、食べられない状態になることだ。湿度や温度など繁殖しやすい環境もあるけど、食品の水分が多くなると微生物は増えやすい、つまり腐りやすい。そこで塩の出番なんだ。

Teacher: This is a topic related to how salt is used as a preservative. To start off, food spoilage happens when microbes inside the food or which came from outside increase and the food becomes inedible. While these microbes easily breed due to moisture and heat, among other things in the environment, it's also very easy for them to increase and for the food to spoil when moisture increases. And this is where salt comes into play.

>　のの　どういうこと？

Nono: What do you mean?

>　先生　まず脱水。料理中に生の野菜や魚に塩をふって、表面から水が出るのを見たことない？　これは細胞の外側を形作る膜の内側と外側で塩分濃度に差があると、同じになろうと薄い方の水が膜を通って濃い方へ移動する性質があるから。「浸透圧」と呼ぶ。

Teacher: First, dehydration. Have you seen water coming out from the surface of vegetables, fish and the like after putting salt while cooking? When there's a difference in the amount of salt in the inner and the outer membranes that form the exterior part of the cell, water, which is thinner, moves toward the denser portion of the membrane. This is called "osmotic pressure."

>　のの　塩が水を引き出すのか。じゃあ塩をたくさんふれば……。

Nono: So salt draws water out. If we put a lot more salt on the surface...

>　先生　差が大きい分、多くの水が移動する。食品の水分が減るから、微生物の使える水も減る。

Teacher: More water will move toward the portion where there's a larger amount of salt. And because water inside the food decreases, the water that is used by microbes also decreases.

>　のの　生の魚より塩サケの身がしまっているのは水分が少ないからか。しょっぱいのは関係ある？

Nono: Salted salmon is more compact than live fish, because it's less moist. Is this in any way related to its saltiness?
