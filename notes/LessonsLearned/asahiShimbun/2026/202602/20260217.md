Hideki Sato, former Sega President and developer of the Sega Saturn, the Dreamcast and other video game consoles, has died

## --

>　佐藤　秀樹さん（さとう・ひでき=元セガ社長）2月13日死去、75歳。

Mr. Hideki Sato (Former Sega President) has died on Feb. 13th at 75 years old.

>　1971年セガ・エンタープライゼス（現セガ）入社。83年に同社が家庭用ゲーム機に進出した際、開発責任者としてゲームパソコン「SC―3000」を設計。それ以降、16ビットCPUを搭載した「メガドライブ」（88年）や格闘ゲーム「バーチャファイター」が人気を博した「セガサターン」（94年）、ネットワーク機能を標準搭載した「ドリームキャスト」（98年）など、セガのすべての家庭用ゲーム機開発に携わった。01年3月、大川功会長兼社長の死去を受けて後任の社長に就いた。03年にセガ会長。

He joined Sega Enterprises (now Sega) in 1971. He was the developer who designed the video game PC, "SC-3000," during the time when the company was going into the home consoles market in 1983. He was later involved in the development of all of Sega's video game consoles from the "Mega Drive (1988)," which had a 16-bit CPU, the "Sega Saturn (1994)," which gained popularity for the fighting game, "Virtua Fighter," the "Dreamcast (1998)," which had a standard networking feature installed, etc. In March 2001, he succeeded Mr. Isao Okawa, the president and chairperson of Sega at the time, as the company's president after the latter passed away. He became Sega's chairperson in 2003.
