> 日本、フィリピンと中古護衛艦の輸出協議へ　解禁後初の案件の可能性

Japan forms an agreement with the Philippines to export to the latter a used escort ship, the first example of a possibility after the lifting of the ban on the export of weapons

> 2026年5月5日 21時49分

2026-05-05T21:49

> マニラ＝佐藤瑞季 加藤あず佐

@Manila, Mizuki Satou and Azusa Kato

## --

>　小泉進次郎防衛相は5日、訪問先のフィリピン・マニラで、テオドロ国防相と会談した。退役させる海上自衛隊の「あぶくま」型護衛艦の輸出に向けて協議に入ることを決めた。高市内閣は4月、殺傷能力のある武器の輸出を全面的に解禁したが、中古護衛艦のフィリピンへの輸出が実現すれば、第1号案件となる可能性がある。

On May 5th, Japanese Minister of Defense Shinjiro Koizumi met with Philippine Defense Secretary Gilbert Teodoro during his visit to Manila, Philippines. They formed an agreement to export the Japanese Maritime Self-Defense Force's "ABUKUMA"-type escort ship. While Takaichi's cabinet lifted the ban on the export of potentially lethal and harmful weapons, fulfilling the export of the used escort ship to the Philippines would be the first example of a possibility of this revision in the Japanese law.

>　政府は4月、武器輸出を規制する防衛装備移転三原則と運用指針を改定。輸出の目的を「救難・輸送・警戒・監視・掃海」に限っていた「5類型」を撤廃し、殺傷能力のある武器の輸出を全面的に解禁した。

The Japanese government updated the previous month the operational guidelines and the three principles on the transfer of defense equipment, which regulated the export of weapons. More specifically, the government abolished the "5 types" permitted in order to export abroad, namely for "Rescue, Transport, Defense/Guard, Surveillance and Minesweeping," thereby completely lifting the ban on the export of potentially lethal and harmful weapons.
